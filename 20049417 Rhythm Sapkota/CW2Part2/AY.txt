Angus McKinnon Young (born March 31, 1955) is an Australian musician best known for co-founding, leading guitarist, composer, and being the only consistent original member of the Australian hard rock band AC/DC. He's noted for his high-energy performances, stage attire inspired by schoolboy uniforms, and his own take on Chuck Berry's duckwalk. Young was voted 24th among the 100 best guitarists of all time by Rolling Stone magazine.
